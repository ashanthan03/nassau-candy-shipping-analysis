## Overview

Nassau Candy Distributor operates a nationwide distribution network, shipping candy products from five manufacturing facilities to customers across the United States. This analysis examined **10,194 shipment orders** across **196 factory-to-state routes** to identify where the distribution network is performing well, where it is falling short, and what specific actions can improve logistics efficiency.

---

## What We Analyzed

- **5 factories** located in Arizona, Georgia, Minnesota, Illinois, and Tennessee
- **15 candy products** across Chocolate, Sugar, and Other divisions
- **4 shipping modes:** Standard Class, Second Class, First Class, Same Day
- **4 geographic regions:** Atlantic, Gulf, Interior, Pacific
- **59 US states and territories** as delivery destinations
- **Orders placed:** January 2024 – December 2025

---

## 5 Key Findings

### 1. Two Factories Handle Nearly All Orders
Lot's O' Nuts (Arizona) and Wicked Choccy's (Georgia) together process **96.6% of all shipments**. This creates a significant supply chain concentration risk — any disruption at either facility would severely impact nationwide distribution.

### 2. Standard Class Shipping is Surprisingly the Fastest
Despite being the lowest-priority shipping classification, Standard Class achieved the **fastest average delivery time** (1,314 days) compared to First Class (1,338 days) and Same Day (1,333 days). This paradox suggests the company may be paying premium shipping costs without receiving premium shipping speed.

### 3. Tennessee Factory Outperforms All Others
The Other Factory in Tennessee delivers the **best average lead time at 1,280 days**, approximately 60 days faster than the slowest facility (Sugar Shack). Its geographic positioning in the Southeast appears to provide routing advantages across the Gulf and Atlantic regions.

### 4. Northeast Routes from Sugar Shack Are the Worst Bottleneck
Routes from Sugar Shack (Minnesota) to northeastern states — specifically New Jersey (1,642 days), Connecticut (1,641 days), and New Hampshire (1,641 days) — represent the most extreme inefficiencies in the entire network, likely due to the extreme geographic distance between Minnesota and the Northeast.

### 5. Washington State is a High-Priority Concern
With **506 orders** and an average lead time of **1,361 days** — 43 days above the company average — Washington state is both a high-volume market and a significantly underperforming delivery destination.

## 3 Priority Recommendations

| Priority | Action | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **High** | Audit why Standard Class outperforms premium shipping modes — renegotiate carrier contracts or route premium shipments differently | Reduce shipping costs and improve high-priority order delivery speed |
| **High** | Establish a regional fulfillment or carrier partnership for Sugar Shack → Northeast routes | Reduce worst-case lead times by an estimated 15–20% |
| **Medium** | Increase capacity at The Other Factory (Tennessee) given its superior performance | Better serve Gulf and Southeast markets while reducing concentration risk |
